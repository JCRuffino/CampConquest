Build the biggest connected group of areas. Areas that touch each other count as next to each other; areas separated by a hatched grey gap do not. Your score is the size of your largest group of linked areas, and the team with the biggest group when the countdown ends wins.

Instant win: if a team ever controls more than half of all areas — locked or not — the game ends immediately and they win.

Both locked and unlocked areas count. Ties are broken by total areas controlled, then by locked areas.

Every area's challenge is secret until your team presses Start Challenge Attempt at that area. Starting reveals the challenge — and commits your team: you must then record either a pass or a fail.

Some challenges are timed — and you won't know until you start! A countdown challenge gives you a fixed window that starts the moment you press Start; a count-up challenge races the clock, and your time is your result. The live timer shows in the area's popup on everyone's phone.

Only press Start when your team is actually at the area and ready to go.

Every challenge has a pass mark — reach it and you claim the area, recording your result (a number, time, distance…) which becomes the score other teams must beat to steal it from you.

Fall short of the pass mark and you must record a fail: your team can't attempt that area's challenge again until another team passes it (by claiming or stealing the area — that resets everyone's fails there).

Teams are on their honour — record claims, results and fails honestly!

Any claimed-but-unlocked area (shown hatched on the map) can be stolen: go there, start the same challenge, and beat the recorded result. The area transfers to your team and is locked — shown solid on the map, it can never be stolen again.

A steal attempt is a duel that settles the area forever: the moment one rival team starts it, the third team is shut out of that area — and if the steal fails, the area locks for the original owner instead.

So a weak result invites a steal, but every steal attempt permanently settles that area, one way or the other. Choose your battles!
